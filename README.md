## Overall Trends
SQL is the most universally demanded skill, appearing in over 50% of Data Analyst and Data Scientist roles and 68% of Data Engineer roles.

Python is the dominant programming language for Data Science and Data Engineering positions.

Cloud and big-data technologies (AWS, Azure, Spark) are primarily associated with Data Engineering roles.

Visualization tools like Tableau are much more important for Data Analysts than for Data Scientists.

## Key insights US,2023
SQL remains the most in-demand skill throughout the year
Consistently leads all other skills with demand ranging from 45%–54%.
Despite a decline toward November, it remains the dominant requirement in job postings.
This reinforces SQL as the foundational skill for Data Analyst roles.

Excel is the second most requested skill
Maintains strong demand around 42%–43% for most of the year.
Experiences a noticeable drop from September to November before recovering in December.
Shows that traditional spreadsheet skills continue to be highly valued alongside modern analytics tools.

Python and Tableau have similar demand levels
Both hover around 25%–30% throughout the year.
Python peaks around August, briefly surpassing Tableau.
Their close trend suggests employers often seek a combination of programming and visualization capabilities.

Python shows the highest growth potential
Demonstrates upward momentum during the middle of the year.
Peaks at approximately 31% in August, indicating increasing adoption of automation, machine learning, and advanced analytics.

Tableau demand remains relatively stable
Fluctuates within a narrow range of 25%–30%.
Indicates steady demand for dashboarding and business intelligence skills.

SAS is the least requested skill
Remains below 22% throughout the year.
Shows a gradual decline with only a slight recovery in December.
Suggests that organizations are increasingly favoring Python and other modern analytics tools over SAS.

November appears to be a low-demand month across most skills
SQL, Excel, Python, Tableau, and SAS all experience declines around November.
This could reflect seasonal hiring slowdowns or reduced job posting activity.

How well do jobs and skills pay for Data Analysts?

![Salary Distribution of Data Jobs in the US](3_Project\images\salary_analysis_datajobs.png)

## Insights
Senior roles have the highest pay bands
Senior Data Scientist and Senior Data Engineer boxes sit furthest to the right, indicating higher typical salaries than non-senior roles.

Data Scientists generally out-earn Data Analysts
The median and interquartile range (the box) for Data Scientist are above those for Data Analyst, suggesting a higher central pay level.

Engineering roles are competitive with science roles
Data Engineer and Senior Data Engineer distributions are close to their Data Scientist counterparts, especially at the senior level, showing strong market value for data infrastructure skills.

High-end outliers are common
All roles have numerous outliers extending well beyond the main distribution. The most extreme are in Data Scientist and Data Engineer, reaching roughly the $500k–$600k range.

Overlap matters more than titles alone
Although senior titles trend higher, the boxes overlap. A high-paying Data Analyst or Data Engineer role can exceed lower-paying Data Scientist roles depending on company, geography, and total compensation structure.

## Insights

High-paying skills differ significantly from the most in-demand skills. Niche technologies such as dplyr ($195K), Bitbucket ($190K), and GitLab ($188K) command the highest salaries, while widely used skills like Python, Tableau, SQL, and Power BI dominate job demand but offer comparatively lower median salaries.

Python remains the strongest all-round skill. It ranks as the most in-demand skill (~$98K median salary), indicating that while it may not yield the highest salaries, it is a foundational requirement for most Data Analyst roles.

Specialized and emerging technologies attract salary premiums. Skills such as Solidity, Hugging Face, Couchbase, and Cassandra are associated with salaries between $145K–$180K, suggesting employers pay more for scarce expertise in AI, blockchain, and advanced data platforms.

Traditional BI and reporting tools remain highly sought after. Tableau, Power BI, Excel, and SQL Server all appear in the top-demand list, highlighting that organizations continue to prioritize data visualization, reporting, and business intelligence capabilities for Data Analyst positions.

## Insights

Python and Tableau offer the highest salary potential among widely demanded skills, with median salaries around $97K–$93K, making them high-value skills for Data Analysts seeking better compensation.

SQL is the most in-demand skill, appearing in nearly 60% of Data Analyst job postings, while still offering a strong median salary of about $91K, highlighting it as a foundational skill for the role.

Traditional office tools such as Word, PowerPoint, and Excel are common but lower-paying, with median salaries ranging from $81K–$85K, suggesting that technical analytics skills (Python, SQL, Tableau, Power BI) drive higher earning potential.
